# First Taste of my new Internship


So, I've finally started my new internship on February 27, 2023. Here's a little summary on how the first few days have been playing out.

## A little background on DBML

So at first, I received a little orientation. I was already familiar with the ![Discovery Bay Marine Lab](https://www.mona.uwi.edu/cms/dbml.htm), so a lot of the orientation was focused on stuff like history. The
genius and mastermind behind this lab was one ![Dr. Thomas Goreau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_F._Goreau) one of the pioneers of diving marine science. He spent a
lot of his life working in the Bay, and received land for the Lab due to his work. While sadly, he passed before its completion, the lab has seen some of the best marine 
and environmental scientists in Jamaica and the wider Caribbean. The lab's main focuses are:
-  Outreach/Teaching
-  Research
 
  
It also has some very prety views if i do say so myself.

### Teaching
On my first day, we prepared for a school which would be coming on the following day. There were a few other interns who had been there a bit longer to show me the ropes.
We cleaned the aquariums for the incoming groups to be able to interact with. We went out into the bay to find samples. I took my borrowed camera and took some pics on the trip.

We didn't carry everything back, but the other interns and I brought enough urchins and cucumbers to make sure the high schoolers had cool stuff to see and touch.

### Research
On my second day. I helped and MSc student with his project. This involved one of my favourite parts of being a marine biologist, scuba diving. On the first day, we had some trouble trying to get his structure into the seabed. We ended up deciding to change our approach and tackle it early the next day.

Later that day, I started some preliminary scouting for my own pet project. As long as I fulfilled my obligations, I was allowed to take on a research pet project. As you might be able to guess, i've taken on another mariculture project with Gracilaria, on my own this time. The same MSc student returned the favour and helped me look around. I also shared a little about the project idea (and my work at Kee Farms.)

That project might get its own post later as it progresses.
