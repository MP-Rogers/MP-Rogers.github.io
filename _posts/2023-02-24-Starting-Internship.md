So, I'm taking on something new for a little while as a marine biologist. I've recently been accepted for an internship at the Discovery Bay Marine Lab in Jamaica. 
This is one of the main marine labs for the country, and many of Jamaica's (and the caribbean's) Scientists have passed through working here. Especially marine biologists.
I am excited, don't get me wrong. But it is still a bit of a new experience. 

Not the lab itself. I've been there plenty of times, even before during my degree. But it will be one of the first times i'm away from my support system. The lab a
solid few hours away from any friends or relatives. 

Now, I don't believe in making things a problem before they become one. I'm sure this will be a great experience. In fact, this internship should give me a ton of new experiences
which means even more adventures in the Sun, Sea and Sand for me to write about. 

In fact, barring some unforeseen circumstance, you can expect:
- A Day in the life of a Marine Biologist
- Solo Seaweed Farming Adventures
- My Advanced Open Water Certification Story
- A little run down of any other projects I'm helping out with
- A dive I'm looking forward to with a friend (and marine biology content creator)
- My first time catching and trying lionfish

I'll still be working with Kee Farms and pursuing other opportunities, but I will be adjusting and giving this internship my best.
